OVERCASTE

Overcaste is a 3D adventure game about a sand-shifter protecting her home from futuristic invading forces. The game is all about brisk and fluid movement through beautiful desert environments, featuring surreal boss battles against monolithic alien oppressors. I was the UI/UX designer from January of 2022 to December of 2023.

UI prototyping and testing

I came into the Overcaste team following my time in Personal Eyes, where the mid-2000s visual theme dictated the UI art. Overcaste, on the other hand, was not as mature in its art direction. I started by designing Overcaste's UI with strong visuals first, but progress was slow because our visual language didn't exist yet.

Then I had a meeting with a mentor who introduced me to the value of designing the User Experience before even thinking of the User Interface. After that, I pivoted to completing the necessary interactions and info presentation with simple shapes, and adjusting them based on playtest feedback. This way of working made iteration much faster.

Working with a professional artist

The most exciting part of Overcaste was working with our team's illustrator. We concepted ideas together and once his art assets were complete, I found ways to animate them in the engine. Some of these assets were static images I had to squash and stretch. Others were animated, and I created materials in Unreal to flip through the frames of the animation.


What we used

Overcaste is being developed with Unreal Engine 4.
